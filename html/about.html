About Chess

Historical Facts

    Chess, one of the world’s most strategic and enduring games, has a rich history that dates back over 1,500 years. It originated in India around the 6th century as a game called Chaturanga, which featured elements resembling modern chess—infantry, cavalry, elephants, and chariots, representing pawns, knights, bishops, and rooks. From there, it spread to Persia, where it became Shatranj, and later to the Islamic world, where scholars refined its rules. By the Middle Ages, chess had reached Europe, evolving into the game we recognize today, with significant rule changes in the 15th century—introducing powerful moves for the queen and bishop—that made the game faster and more dynamic. By the 19th century, chess had become a global intellectual battleground, leading to the first official World Chess Championship in 1886. Today, with computers and artificial intelligence challenging even the best human minds, chess continues to evolve, proving itself as a timeless game of strategy, patience, and mastery.
